Scientific Activities

The ASC is open to proposals for scientific activities including workshops, thematic programs, conferences and schools. Generally, they should consist of a period of time up to three months of concentrated activity in a specific area of current research in theoretical physics. Typically, they should include opportunities for engaging graduate students, such as graduate courses and easy access to long-term visitors.

The proposal shall be sent per email and must contain the following information:

  • Names and affiliations of the organizing committee, with full contact information for each member
  • Estimated dates and locations of the activities
  • Overview of the topic including description of the topic, scientific background of the topic, recent progress and/or actual status
  • Argument about why such a program should be run
  • Outline of the proposed program
  • Proposed graduate courses, with names and affiliations of instructors
  • Expected budget
  • Names and affiliations of proposed short- and long-term participants,
  • Curriculum vitae for each proposed organizing committee member,
  • Names and contact information of two potential referees.

Sommerfeld Lecture Series

The Sommerfeld Lecture Series brings renowned theoretical physics from around the world to the ASC to give 3 lectures. The first one oriented to a general audience, the second intended as a colloquium, and the third for a specialized public.

The ASC designates lectureres based on their outstanding contributions to the advancemenet of theoretical physics, excellence in research and strong presentation skills.

Proposals for Lecturers

The proposals for lecturers should be sent to this email and must contain:
